Background

Context & Situation

We surveyed 310 university students (18–25 yrs) and conducted 5 in-depth interviews to understand how young adults navigate unexplained experiences and beliefs in black magic.

Many sit in a "gray zone" between scientific education and cultural tradition, often facing distress without integrated support systems that honor both realities.

User Research

Pain Points & Needs

Combined Pain Points

  • 1.Blind belief in supernatural traditions and rituals
  • 2.Generational/community fear and social pressure
  • 3.Anxiety, stress, emotional insecurity and fear of curses
  • 4.Dependence on supernatural explanations during problems
  • 5.Misinterpreting coincidences due to confirmation bias
  • 6.Influence of horror/paranormal media and misinformation
  • 7.Lack of scientific awareness and critical thinking
  • 8.Emotional dependence on rituals and spiritual healers

Combined Needs

  • 1.Scientific awareness, rational understanding, evidence-based thinking
  • 2.Critical thinking, logical reasoning, and psychology/cognitive bias education
  • 3.Mental health awareness, counseling, stress management, safe emotional support
  • 4.Open family/community discussions and supportive guidance networks
  • 5.Media literacy and awareness of misinformation/superstition

5 Whys Analysis

Root Causes

Problem Statement

Young adults experiencing supernatural distress have no safe, effective support system

↓
Why #1 — Cultural & Traditional Influence

Family, religion, and community traditions normalize supernatural beliefs without scientific grounding

↓
Why #2 — Fear & Emotional Vulnerability

Stress, anxiety, and fear of the unknown encourage supernatural explanations over psychological ones

↓
Why #3 — Media Influence & Low Scientific Awareness

Paranormal media and limited scientific understanding strengthen irrational beliefs

↓
Why #4 — Social Reinforcement

Community influence and spiritual healers reinforce belief and provide emotional comfort

↓
🎯 Root Cause

No culturally-sensitive platforms exist that distinguish legitimate practices from exploitation, explain psychological mechanisms accessibly, provide integrated support pathways, and create safe spaces for the "gray zone" of uncertainty.

Learnings

Conclusion

Understanding the Gray Zone

Young adults don't exist in binary states of belief or disbelief. Most occupy a complex "gray zone" where scientific education coexists with cultural traditions, creating unique psychological tensions that require nuanced, non-judgmental support.

Empathy-First Technology

MindBridge demonstrates that effective mental health technology doesn't require massive infrastructure. Through careful prompt engineering, a free LLM API can deliver culturally-sensitive, psychologically-informed support at scale.

Bridging Two Worlds

The solution must honor both scientific understanding and lived cultural experience. Dismissing either worldview alienates users. The future of mental health support in India requires frameworks that hold both simultaneously.

Safety & Ethics First

Hard-coded crisis escalation, privacy-first design, and practitioner verification are non-negotiable. Technology in this space carries a duty of care that must be designed in from the start, not added later.
