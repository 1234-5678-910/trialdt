User / Protagonist

Our primary persona represents the typical user we're designing for.

"Akhil"

The Skeptic Turned Believer

Age: 20 years

Engineering Student

Urban Campus

Science-Minded

User Story

"I don't know what I believe anymore. It sits in this uncomfortable middle space."

Background

Akhil is a 20-year-old engineering student who considers himself science-minded and didn't believe in black magic. However, after participating in a ritual to "prove it was fake," he experienced a blackout and 14-16 hours of psychological distress. Medical treatment (anti-depressants) didn't fully help, and while he recovered in 10-15 days, he remains uncertain about what happened.

Key Pain Points
  • Cognitive dissonance between scientific beliefs and unexplained personal experience
  • Medical treatment didn't fully address his psychological distress
  • Lack of integrated support systems that respect both scientific and cultural perspectives
  • Social isolation - unable to discuss experiences without judgment
Needs
  • Integrated support that respects both scientific and cultural perspectives
  • Immediate grounding techniques for moments of distress
  • Safe, anonymous space to discuss experiences without judgment
  • Access to both psychological and cultural support resources

Understanding the landscape of black magic beliefs in India and their impact on young adults.

Research Scope

Interview Topics:

  • • Personal experiences with unexplained phenomena
  • • Belief formation and sources
  • • Mental health impact
  • • Help-seeking behavior
  • • Cultural and family influences

Key Cultural Tension

Young Indians are navigating a complex landscape of family traditions, social media influence, AND scientific education—often without integrated frameworks to process unexplained experiences. This creates cognitive dissonance, anxiety, and a lack of appropriate support systems.

Black Magic in India: More Than Folklore

Black magic in India is more than folklore—it contributes to crime, fraud, violence, and psychological distress. Surveys show many people believe in its effects, often influenced by tradition and media, while court cases and regional reports highlight growing legal concerns. Although belief remains common, many victims seek medical or psychological help, linking superstition with mental health. Social spread of victimhood claims further amplifies harm, emphasizing the need for legal, educational, and psychological interventions that respect cultural contexts.

Root Causes (5 Whys)

Deep dive analysis to uncover the underlying causes of supernatural belief-related distress.

P

Problem Statement

Young adults experience psychological distress from unexplained experiences but lack accessible, non-judgmental support systems that integrate scientific and cultural perspectives.

1

Why #1: Cultural & Traditional Influence

Family, religion, and community traditions normalize supernatural beliefs, creating a foundation where supernatural explanations are readily accepted.

Evidence: 28.4% of respondents cite Family/Tradition as primary source of belief

2

Why #2: Fear & Emotional Vulnerability

Stress, anxiety, and fear of the unknown encourage supernatural explanations as coping mechanisms during times of uncertainty.

Evidence: 58% report mental health impact from supernatural beliefs

3

Why #3: Personal Experiences & Cognitive Bias

Coincidences and unusual events are linked to supernatural beliefs through confirmation bias and pattern recognition, reinforcing existing beliefs.

Evidence: 45.8% report unexplained experiences; 68% of believers had such experiences

4

Why #4: Media Influence & Low Scientific Awareness

Paranormal media and limited scientific understanding strengthen irrational beliefs, while social media amplifies fear and spreads misinformation.

Evidence: 18.7% cite Internet/Social Media as belief source; 13.5% cite Movies/Media

5

ROOT CAUSE: Social Reinforcement & Spiritual Dependence

Community influence and spiritual healers reinforce belief and provide emotional comfort, creating a self-sustaining cycle. The lack of integrated frameworks that respect both cultural traditions and scientific understanding leaves users without appropriate support systems.

Key Insight:

  • • 54.5% want medical OR combined support
  • • 21.6% want both approaches
  • • Fragmented support systems force users to choose sides
  • • No culturally-sensitive platforms exist to bridge this gap

3

Features include sharing experiences, understanding emotions, verified support, and calming audio guides.

4

The bot validates beliefs respectfully while providing psychological and cultural insights.

5

6

Conclusion & Learnings

Key insights and takeaways from our Design Thinking journey.

What We Learned

Understanding the Gray Zone

Young adults don't exist in binary states of belief or disbelief. Most occupy a complex "gray zone" where scientific education coexists with cultural traditions, creating unique psychological tensions that require nuanced, non-judgmental support.
